About Me


Albert Einstein

                          Greetings, curious minds! I am Albert Einstein , and it is both an honor and a pleasure to welcome you to my virtual abode. Within these digital walls, I hope to share a glimpse into the musings of a humble physicist and philosopher. Born on March 14, 1879, in the quaint town of Ulm, Germany, I embarked on a lifelong journey to unravel the mysteries of the universe. My passion for physics led me to develop the groundbreaking theory of relativity, forever altering our understanding of space, time, and gravity. The equation E=mc^2 became not only a symbol of scientific brilliance but a testament to the beauty and elegance inherent in the laws governing our cosmos.

Beyond the scientific realm, I am known for my philosophical and humanitarian views. I advocate for pacifism, civil rights, and global cooperation. My belief in the importance of curiosity and imagination extends beyond the scientific method into all aspects of life. I received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for my explanation of the photoelectric effect, contributing significantly to the development of quantum theory. Read more here on Wikipedia.

Quantum Theory of Light

Albert Einstein made his mark in 1905 by formulating a startling new theory of light. He did this by expanding upon Planck’s idea of quantization to conjecture that light itself is made up of discrete packets or ‘quanta’. Each of these quanta contained a fixed amount of energy directly related to the light’s frequency. Their name? Photons! Read More

Special Theory of Relativity

Albert Einstein's 1905 theory of special relativity is an explanation of how speed affects mass, time and space. The theory includes a way for the speed of light to define the relationship between energy and matter small amounts of mass (m) can be interchangeable with enormous amounts of energy (E), as defined by the classic equation E = mc^2. Photoelectric Effect

In 1905, Albert Einstein published a paper on the photoelectric effect. The photoelectric effect is when light hits a material and releases electrons. Einstein's paper proposed that light is made up of tiny particles called quanta. This led to the development of quantum mechanics. Einstein's discovery won him a Nobel Prize in 1921. Read More
